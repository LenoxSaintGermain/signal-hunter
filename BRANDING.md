# Branding & Naming Options

## Repository Name Candidates

### Top Recommendations from Claude

1. **AcquireIQ** ⭐ RECOMMENDED
   - Rationale: Combines "acquire" with "IQ" to convey intelligence in acquisitions
   - Domain: acquireiq.com (check availability)
   - Pros: Clear, memorable, conveys intelligence
   - Cons: Common pattern (-IQ suffix)

2. **Dealvantage**
   - Rationale: Combines "deal" and "advantage" to suggest a competitive edge
   - Domain: dealvantage.com
   - Pros: Unique, conveys competitive positioning
   - Cons: Slightly harder to spell

3. **Acquira**
   - Rationale: Short, memorable name that implies acquiring and achieving
   - Domain: acquira.com
   - Pros: Short, elegant, easy to remember
   - Cons: May sound like "acquire-a" (unclear)

4. **Precideal**
   - Rationale: Combines "precise" and "deal" to emphasize accuracy in deal-making
   - Domain: precideal.com
   - Pros: Unique, emphasizes precision
   - Cons: Harder to pronounce

5. **Acquision**
   - Rationale: Blend of "acquisition" and "vision" to suggest strategic foresight
   - Domain: acquision.com
   - Pros: Conveys strategic thinking
   - Cons: Looks like misspelling of "acquisition"

6. **Dealytic**
   - Rationale: Combines "deal" and "analytic" to highlight data-driven decision-making
   - Domain: dealytic.com
   - Pros: Modern, tech-forward
   - Cons: May be confused with "dialectic"

7. **Acqpower**
   - Rationale: Combines "acquisition" and "power" to convey strength and capability
   - Domain: acqpower.com
   - Pros: Strong, powerful positioning
   - Cons: Harder to spell/remember

8. **Acquirium**
   - Rationale: Unique, memorable name that suggests a place for acquisitions
   - Domain: acquirium.com
   - Pros: Very unique, memorable
   - Cons: Unclear meaning

9. **Dealgenius**
   - Rationale: Combines "deal" and "genius" to emphasize intelligent deal-making
   - Domain: dealgenius.com
   - Pros: Clear value proposition
   - Cons: May sound boastful

10. **Acquipoint**
    - Rationale: Combines "acquire" and "point" to suggest precision in acquisitions
    - Domain: acquipoint.com
    - Pros: Professional, precise
    - Cons: Less memorable

## Alternative Positioning Options

### Option 1: "Million Hunter" (Current)
- Pros: Bold, memorable, conveys ambition
- Cons: May sound informal for institutional investors
- Best for: Acquisition entrepreneurs, individual investors

### Option 2: "AcquireIQ" (Recommended)
- Pros: Professional, intelligent, scalable
- Cons: Less distinctive than "Million Hunter"
- Best for: Institutional investors, PE firms, professional acquirers

### Option 3: "DealCommand"
- Pros: Clear value prop, military precision connotation
- Cons: Generic, may be taken
- Best for: Operational focus, command center positioning

### Option 4: "Apex Acquisitions Platform"
- Pros: Premium positioning, clear category
- Cons: Long, generic
- Best for: Enterprise sales

## Brand Voice Guidelines

### Tone
- **Intelligent**: Data-driven, analytical, precise
- **Powerful**: Confident, authoritative, commanding
- **Sophisticated**: Premium, refined, professional
- **Innovative**: Forward-thinking, AI-powered, cutting-edge

### Writing Style
- Use active voice and strong verbs
- Lead with benefits, not features
- Quantify value where possible
- Avoid jargon, but use industry terminology
- Balance aspiration with credibility

### Visual Identity
- **Colors**: Deep blues (trust, intelligence), accent gold (premium, success)
- **Typography**: Inter (current) - modern, readable, professional
- **Imagery**: Clean, minimal, data-focused
- **UI Style**: Apple-inspired - generous whitespace, smooth animations, glass morphism
